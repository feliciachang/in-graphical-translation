In Graphical Translation


Experiments with Text and Image by Felicia Chang


About

My design professor, Pamela Hovland once described type as a container. What information can typography hold besides content?

How can we derive a more direct relationship between type and the space in which it sits? Can they speak the same visual language, share the same formal qualities?

These questions culminated in two image-making tools that seek to translate between the two elements we use most in design: type and image.

See Full Report | Code


Image as Type

Type as Image is a tool that extracts shapes, particularly rectangles and circles from images to create endlessly generative letterforms

Try the editor

Type as Image

Image as Type is a typographic projection tool. 2D images are converted to 3D models. Text can then be projected onto a sequence of images.

Examples

The Making of Nature

The Making of Nature is a chapbook that combines my collection of leaf images with Joyce Carol Oates' "Against Nature." During the semester, I collected leaves on my walks to Covid-19 tests twice a week.

In the midst of this act of nature appreciation, I read "Against Nature" which portrays nature in its cruel, honest form. I wanted to subvert my own work by "glitching" in the same way Joyce Carol Oates removes us from our conventional understanding of nature.




Cacophany

Cacophany is a short film that explores a narrative told by sentence pairs, sound and image. Daily domestic life is juxtaposed with a variety of bird calls, collected from the National Park Service database.

This juxtaposition is meant to demonstrate how society removes us from nature, and thus the death that arises from climate change.
